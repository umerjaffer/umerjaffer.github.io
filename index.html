How to Use Each Calculator

What is X% of Y?

Formula: (X/100) × Y

This calculator helps you find a percentage of any number. For example, calculating a 15% tip on a $50 bill.

Example: What is 15% of 200?

Solution: (15/100) × 200 = 30

X is what % of Y?

Formula: (X/Y) × 100

Use this to find what percentage one number is of another. Perfect for calculating success rates or proportions.

Example: 25 is what percent of 200?

Solution: (25/200) × 100 = 12.5%

Percentage Increase/Decrease

Formula: ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100

Calculate how much a value has increased or decreased in percentage terms. Useful for tracking growth or decline.

Example: Price increased from $100 to $150

Solution: ((150 - 100) / 100) × 100 = 50% increase

Percentage Change

Formula: Original Value ± (Original Value × Percentage/100)

Find the new value after applying a percentage increase or decrease. Great for calculating discounts or markups.

Example: Increase 200 by 25%

Solution: 200 + (200 × 25/100) = 250

Percentage to Fraction

Formula: Percentage ÷ 100, then simplify

Convert percentages to their simplest fraction form. Useful for mathematical calculations and comparisons.

Example: Convert 75% to fraction

Solution: 75% = 75/100 = 3/4

Fraction to Percentage

Formula: (Numerator ÷ Denominator) × 100

Convert fractions to their percentage equivalent. Helpful for understanding proportions in percentage terms.

Example: Convert 3/4 to percentage

Solution: (3 ÷ 4) × 100 = 75%
