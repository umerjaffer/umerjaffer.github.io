How to Calculate What Percent X is of Y

Formula

(X/Y) × 100

To find what percentage X is of Y, divide X by Y and multiply by 100. This calculation shows what portion or percentage X represents when compared to Y as a whole.

Step-by-Step Example

Example: What percent is 30 of 150?
  1. Identify your values:
    • X = 30 (the part you're comparing)
    • Y = 150 (the total or whole amount)
  2. Divide X by Y:
    30 ÷ 150 = 0.2
  3. Multiply by 100:
    0.2 × 100 = 20
  4. Therefore, 30 is 20% of 150

Common Use Cases

  • Academic performance (correct answers out of total questions)
  • Business metrics (sales achieved vs sales target)
  • Progress tracking (tasks completed vs total tasks)
  • Budget analysis (amount spent vs total budget)
  • Health statistics (success rates, recovery percentages)
  • Sports statistics (win rates, accuracy percentages)

Tips for Accurate Calculations

  • Ensure X (part) is smaller than or equal to Y (whole)
  • Keep track of decimal places for precise results
  • Convert fractions to decimals before calculating
  • Verify your answer makes logical sense
  • Consider using rounded numbers for simpler calculations
