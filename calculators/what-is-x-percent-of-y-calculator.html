How to Calculate What Percentage X is of Y

Formula

(X/Y) × 100

To calculate what percentage X is of Y, divide X by Y and multiply by 100. This gives you the percentage that X represents of Y. The result shows what portion of Y is represented by X in percentage form.

Step-by-Step Example

Example: What percentage is 25 of 200?
  1. Identify your values:
    • X = 25 (the number you want to convert to a percentage)
    • Y = 200 (the total or base number)
  2. Divide X by Y:
    25 ÷ 200 = 0.125
  3. Multiply by 100:
    0.125 × 100 = 12.5
  4. Therefore, 25 is 12.5% of 200

Common Use Cases

  • Calculating test scores (correct answers out of total questions)
  • Determining market share (company's sales out of total market sales)
  • Analyzing proportions (part of a whole measurements)
  • Comparing values (relative size comparisons)
  • Financial calculations (profit margins, tax rates)
  • Statistical analysis (population percentages)

Tips for Accurate Calculations

  • Always use the number you're comparing (X) as the numerator
  • The total or base number (Y) should be the denominator
  • Multiply by 100 after division, not before
  • Round your final answer to a reasonable number of decimal places
  • Double-check your decimal point placement
